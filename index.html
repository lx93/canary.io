    How it all started

    Our Story

    Meeting in the sleepy town of Amherst, Massachusetts, Garrette and Hayden began mining DOGE and Bitcoin on the computers of Hampshire College in the fall of 2011. Unbeknownst to them, Gavin Andresen had written most of the Bitcoin code in an office right down the street a couple years earlier.

    In 2015, the Decentralized Autonomous Organization made it clear that smart contracts (and in this case, Ethereum) had the power to revolutionize capital raising - arguably the most significant hurdle in venture creation. Seeing the vast possibilities before their eyes, Garrette and Hayden had no choice but to pool their capital and generate as much hashpower as possible to support the decentralized redistribution of economic incentivization. Since then, Garrette and Hayden have been working diligently to support mining operations all around the world, generating terahashes of mining power in the process.
